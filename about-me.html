ABOUT

I guess I found my love for photography partly due to my love for travel. Travelling & absorbing the intricacies of diverse cultures around the world is a passion of mine. To embrace the diversity in a culture, over a reasonable period of time & evolve my view from "This is Extremely Strange" to “Of course this is normal!” is a mind opening process. Capturing this on an image gives you the opportunity to save that moment in a capsule of time

I often try to emphasise the power of nature in my images & to show no matter how much we manage to mess things it up, nature has got it all under control.


Just to take a wonder possibly after meditation & take random photo's if something catches my eye or impacts on my emotions, I may not know at that time or ever what it was that captured me.

I particularly enjoy watching the birds go about there day to day routines, to try to learn the different attributes & characteristics about a variety of different bird familys. I strive to be in nature in nature as much as possible, as the great Leonardo da vinci would say
“Nature is the source of all true knowledge. She has her own logic, her own laws, she has no effect without cause nor invention without necessity.”
“The wisest & nobelist teacher is nature itself.”


I am in many ways what is called a late bloomer, it may have taken time to find my passions but I learned so much from a variety of perspectives along the path to finding them.


Extremely interested in new technology & the endless opportunities to create anew. I only buy presents that will bring long term value to some ones life, it must have an uplifting use case, preferably long-term benifits.

I realised for me to take a good photo, I need to quieten the mind of thoughts like “I Have to do this, I Have to get that” & to clear any blockages from awareness making for a clean unhindered perspective to view the world around me. I do this through meditation, generally in the early morning. Meditation also helps with living in the moment & photography in many ways is trying to capture a fleeting moment in time. Which to be seen properly it may be necessary to wipe clean a stained glass view.

I was particularly drawn to photography when I stepped away from the television. No more sad news & advertisements to distract me from the beauty that surrounds me. Too many sunrises & sunsets missed catching up on the latest series. So I decided to buy a camera to share what I was witnessing.



I love to look around me & find the beauty in nature, by finding a different perspective. Focusing on it without distractions, this can open you up to see its true beauty


I guess my mission from the beginning was to put a sharp focus on the beauty of the world in times when there are so many distractions to deviate your perspective from seeing what is around you. To shine a light on the incredible ever changing world, away from the media to the greatest artist that is nature


I am self trained photographer, who tries to capture the beauty of passing moments. It's like finding light in a dark place, if you are not looking for it, you may not find it.

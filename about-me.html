ABOUT

I love to travel, I guess this is where my love for photography originated. Travelling & absorbing the intricacies of diverse cultures is a passion of mine. Embracing the diversity over a reasonable period of time evolve in my view from "This is Extremely Strange"to “Of course this is normal!” is a mind opening process. Therefore capturing this on an image is saving that moment in a capsule of time? (image abroad + nepal)


I try to emphasise the power of nature in my images & to show no matter how much we manage to mess it up, nature has got it all under control. (flowers growing out of wall)


I like to take random photo's when something catches my attention (random image). I paricular enjoy watching the birds go about there day to day routines, to try to learn the different attributes & characteristics about a variety of distnguished bird familys. I strive to be in nature in nature as much as possible, as the great Leonardo da vinci would say
“Nature is the source of all true knowledge. She has her own logic, her own laws, she has no effect without cause nor invention without necessity.”
“The wisest & nobelist teacher is nature itself.”*(bird images)


I am in many ways I am what is called a late bloomer, it may have taken time to find my passions but I learned so much from a variety of perspectives along the path to finding them.


Extremely interested in technology & the endless opportunities left to create anew. I only buy presents that will bring long term value to some ones life, it must have an uplifting use case, preferably long-term benifits.


I realised for me to take a good photo, I need to quieten those the mind of “I Have to do this, I Have to do that” & to clear any blockages from awareness making for a clean unhindered perspective to view the world from. (image of dandelion) I do this through meditation, generally in the early morning. Meditation also helps with living in the moment & photography in many ways is trying to capture a fleeting moment in time. Which to be seen properly it may be necessary to wipe clean a stained glass view.


I was particularly drawn to photography when I stepped away from the television. No more sad news & advertisements to distract me from the beauty that surrounds me. Too many sunrises & sunsets missed catching up on ""Lost"". So I decided to buy a camera to share what I was witnessing""" outside my window??"".


(Use the ducks image to portray what you are witnessing & explaining on it through the use of a mirror)

I love to look around me & find the beauty in nature, by finding a different perspective, focusing on it without distractions, this can open your viewpoint to its true beauty (images of flowers/birds)


I guess my mission from the beginning was to put a sharp focus on the beauty of the world in times when there are so many distractions to deviate your perspective? from truly seeing. To shine a light on the incredible ever changing world that surrounds us, away from the media to the artist that is nature??


I am self trained photographer, who tries to capture the beauty of passing moments. It's like finding light in a dark place, if you are not looking for it, you may not find it.
